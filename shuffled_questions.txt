**1**
Which of the following diseases is NOT typically transmitted through contaminated food and water?
A) Poliomyelitis
B) Hepatitis A
C) Cholera
D) Hepatitis C
**Rationale:** D — Keep in mind that Hepatitis C is a bloodborne pathogen, whereas Hepatitis A, Cholera, and Polio primarily spread through the fecal-oral route via contaminated food or water.

A) Cod liver oil
B) Egg yolk
C) Liver
D) Cheese
**Rationale:** A — While egg yolks and cheese contain trace amounts, fish liver oils like cod liver oil are absolutely packed with Vitamin D, making them one of the most potent natural dietary sources available.

**3**
Which of the following laboratory tests is considered the most sensitive indicator for detecting early iron deficiency in a client?
A) Serum ferritin
B) Serum iron
C) Ferrous sulphate levels
D) Hemoglobin
**Rationale:** A — Serum ferritin directly measures the body's actual stored iron reserves, making it a much more sensitive and early indicator of an impending deficiency than waiting for a late-stage drop in hemoglobin.

**4**

A) Tracking the epidemiological surveillance of a disease
B) Determining the incidence of a disease condition within a population
C) Determining the probability of an offspring inheriting a particular genotype
D) Predicting the clinical outcome of a progressive disease
**Rationale:** C — A Punnett square is a foundational genetic diagram used to rapidly calculate the mathematical probability of a child inheriting specific alleles from a maternal and paternal cross.

**15**

Contrast this with convalescent carriers, who spread the illness while recovering, or asymptomatic carriers, who never show signs at all.
